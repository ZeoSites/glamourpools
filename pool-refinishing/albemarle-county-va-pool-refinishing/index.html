Common Pool Issues and Solutions

In pool usage and management, typical pool issues are apt to arise, they are difficulties that happen in situations where a shared or common resource is used by multiple individuals or groups.

Surface Cracks and Damage

Cracks and damage to the pool surface are common, affecting the pool's aesthetics and safety. When this happens, meticulously assess the pool surface, repair any cracks promptly, and consider resurfacing using durable and crack-resistant materials like epoxy or fiberglass coatings.

Fading and Discoloration

The pool's finish can fade or discolor due to exposure to UV rays, chemicals, or aging. Choose high-quality, UV-resistant resurfacing materials and consider additives that prevent discoloration. Maintain proper water chemistry to decrease chemical impact on the surface.

Adhesion Problems (Peeling or Delamination.

Insufficient attachment of the resurfacing material can lead to peeling or delamination over time. Ensure correct surface preparation, including comprehensive cleaning and prepping of the existing surface, and follow the manufacturer's guidelines for material application to improve adhesion.

Algae Growth and Staining

Algae growth and staining can occur on the pool surface, making it unattractive and hard to keep clean. You can opt for algae-resistant resurfacing materials and ensure correct chemical balance and regular cleaning to avoid algae growth and staining.

Rough or Uneven Surface

Resurfacing can sometimes result in an uneven or rough texture, affecting the pool's aesthetic and comfort.
